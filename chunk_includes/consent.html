Information Sheet

Sentence Continuation Study: HUM00176356

Principal Investigator: Lisa Levinson, PhD, University of Michigan

Study Sponsor: University of Michigan UROP

You are invited to participate in a research study about how native speakers respond to sentences of different kinds. This is important to further our understanding of the mind and the language capacity more generally.

If you agree to be part of the research study, you will be asked to:

  • Provide demographic information about your age, gender, and linguistic background.
  • Read a series of sentences, one word at a time, and indicate with keyboard responses which word is the best continuation of the sentence.

Benefits of the research: The possible benefit to you for participating in the research study is that it may make you more aware of how knowledge is discovered in psychology and linguistics.

Risks and discomforts: There are no known risks associated with your participation in this research beyond those of everyday life. To minimize the risk of a breach of confidentiality, we will not collect personal identifying information. When the results of this research are published, discussed in conferences, or shared on open science platforms, no information will be included that personally identifies you.

Compensation: If you participate and complete the study successfully, you will receive payment via the Prolific site for your participation. The rate is specified on the Prolific site, and is pro-rated for parts of an hour. The study is expected to take about 20 minutes.

Participating in this study is completely voluntary. Even if you decide to participate now, you may change your mind and stop at any time. You may choose not to continue with the survey for any reason.

If you have questions about this research study, please contact Dr. Lisa Levinson, lisalev@umich.edu.

The University of Michigan Institutional Review Board Health Sciences and Behavioral Sciences has determined that this study is exempt from IRB oversight.
